<objective>
Pod side (SEED-019 part 2): make the pod self-decide whether its whisper runs on GPU based on detected
VRAM and self-report that device on an already-forwarded channel the gateway (Plan 14-01) reads.

Three edits to the gateway-built bash + the build-baked supervisord conf:
1. primaryOnstartHead (onstart.go) gains an nvidia-smi VRAM probe that exports the whisper device.
2. supervisord.conf:46 drops the hardcoded WHISPER__INFERENCE_DEVICE="cpu" pin (keeps HF_HUB_CACHE) so
   the inherited env wins (env inheritance Option A — fail-open to speaches default).
3. The onstart stands a minimal device-report responder on container port 9100 and buildCreateRequest
   forwards -p 9100:9100, fulfilling the device-report contract Plan 14-01 reads.

Purpose: the pod is the source of truth for its whisper placement; gateway stays shape-agnostic.

Output: a primary-pod onstart that picks cuda on >=30GB shapes and cpu on 24GB, plus a :9100
/whisper_device endpoint the gateway parses. The rebuilt image rollout is gated in Plan 14-03.

CHANNEL DECISION (D-14-01, blocker resolution): the CONTEXT-named health-bridge :9100/health/ready
channel is NOT viable in PROD — the health-bridge is local-dev/docker-compose ONLY (supervisord.conf:9
excludes it; pod/primary/Dockerfile never COPYs its binary; the gateway forwards only 8000/8001/8003/9400).
Rather than import the whole health-bridge (new Dockerfile stage + supervisord child + its compose-style
upstream URLs), this plan stands a one-process static responder on :9100 launched from onstart bash.
Minimal PROD surface, honors CONTEXT intent (pod self-reports device; gateway reads at Ready). Evidence:
14-RESEARCH OPEN Q3 + the supervisord.conf/Dockerfile reads recorded during planning.
