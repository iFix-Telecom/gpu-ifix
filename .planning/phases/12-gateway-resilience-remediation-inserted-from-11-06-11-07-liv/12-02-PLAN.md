<objective>
RES-11: make the primary reconciler detect a dead pod on the steady-state Ready tick (it currently never polls Vast — FINDING 2), port the proven 3-strike `IsTerminal()` + `ErrInstanceNotFound` confirm from `waitForReadyOrDestroy` into `evaluateReady`, advance Ready→Draining→Asleep with BestEffortDestroy, force-open the `local-*` breakers deterministically (D-04), and fire a distinct critical alert for billing-stop vs host-yank (D-03). Fix the empty-trackedID bug (D-05) FIRST in this plan — it is the load-bearing prerequisite that defeated the 11-07 reproduction (Pitfall 1). Add a durable billing-stop suppression marker so a zero-credit pod death does NOT trigger an immediate re-provision loop (Codex HIGH — D-01's "no provision-fail loop" made concrete). Finally, close the symmetric half of the breaker control: `markReady` force-CLOSES the stale `local-*` breakers when a new pod goes Ready (D-13), so a re-provisioned pod never inherits an OPEN breaker left over from probing the dead URL.

Purpose: This is the core autonomous-failover fix. Without it, the prod gateway stays `ready` for 25+ minutes pointing at a dead address. With it, death is confirmed in ~15s, the breakers open, RES-13 (Plan 03) instantly falls traffic to tier-1, and the operator gets an actionable alert. The billing-stop suppression marker prevents the zero-credit provision-fail loop during a peak window. The D-13 force-close completes the loop: when recovery provisions a fresh pod, its breakers are reset to CLOSED so traffic returns to the live pod immediately instead of staying parked on tier-1.

Output: Ready-tick death detection, FSM-driven breaker force-open, distinct billing-stop alert, D-05 trackedID repair, billing-stop suppression marker (schedule-driven re-provision skipped for zero-credit), and D-13 markReady force-close of stale local-* breakers.
</objective>
