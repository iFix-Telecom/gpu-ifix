# Phase 6.6.Y Plan 03: Pod Cold-Start Fix (Option A + B) Summary

**RFC1918-advertising Vast offers are now dropped client-side at the primary pick (toggle default true), and the previously-silent 17-min running-but-unreachable wait is replaced by a per-poll-logged 120s budget-bounded fail-fast that records closure_reason `public_port_bind_timeout` and BestEffortDestroy's the orphan.**

- **Files modified:** 4

## Accomplishments
- **Option A (offer-side RFC1918 reject):** `isRFC1918` + `RejectPrivateIPOffers` in `vast/types.go`, plus `Offer.PublicIPAddr`. The reconciler applies `r.rejectPrivateIPOffers` on BOTH the allowlist and broaden passes, before `FilterBelowCap`, guarded by `cfg.PrimaryVastRejectPrivateIP`. No Vast search-filter-map clause for `public_ipaddr` (finding #4 — grep returns 0).
- **Option B (reconciler health-gate fail-fast):** the silent empty-URLs `continue` in `waitForReadyOrDestroy` now emits a per-poll `log.Warn` (lifecycle_id, vast_instance_id, actual_status, ssh_host, public_ipaddr, elapsed_since_running_s, budget_s) and fails fast once contiguous running time `>=` `PrimaryPublicPortBindBudgetSeconds` — running `BestEffortDestroy` + `closeLifecycle(..., "public_port_bind_timeout", 0)` + returning an error.
- **Deterministic timeout test (finding #7):** `primaryInstancePollIntervalForTest` (5ms) + `PrimaryPublicPortBindBudgetSeconds=0` makes the fail-fast fire on the first running poll; the full primary suite stays well under 30s.
- SPIKE-EVIDENCE caveat recorded verbatim in the reconciler call-site comment: Option A guards the RFC1918 subclass ONLY; neither observed failing host advertised an RFC1918 IP, so Option B is the primary runtime catch.
